Our Story

Four Generations
of Mastery

In 1904, in a modest kitchen on the bustling streets of Shanghai, the first Yulongfu dumpling was folded — a single xiaolongbao bearing 16 precise pleats, each one a promise of perfection.

Today, that promise lives on in Melbourne through Yong and Emily Liu, a husband-and-wife duo who left corporate careers to preserve what their family had meticulously guarded for over a century.

Every recipe is drawn from the original 1904 family archives. Every dumpling is hand-folded to the exacting standards of the 16-pleat fold — a technique so precise it takes years to master.

From the iconic black truffle xiaolongbao wrapped in its signature ink-black dough, to the slow-braised pork belly that simmers for hours, each dish is a chapter in a story that began over 120 years ago.
